Can You Spot the Scam?

Scammers target seniors with fake texts, emails, pop-ups, and phone calls. Read each one, decide if it's safe or a scam, then learn the warning signs. When in doubt, slow down — real companies never rush you.

1

Texts & Emails

The messages that land on your phone and in your inbox.

4 examples
Text message
+1 (402) 555-0148
USPS: Your package is on hold. A $1.99 redelivery fee is required. Pay now: http://usps-redeliver.xyz/pay
Real carriers don't ask for a fee by text with a link — and the web address isn't usps.com. The urgency is the trick. Don't tap it.
Email
Amazon Security <no-reply@amaz0n-verify.com>
Your account has been locked. Verify your details within 24 hours or your account will be permanently deleted. [Verify Now]
“Act within 24 hours or else” is classic pressure, and the sender is “amaz0n-verify.com,” not amazon.com. Don't click — open the Amazon app or type amazon.com yourself.
Email
First Community Bank
Your monthly statement is ready. To view it, log in to your account through our app or website. We'll never ask for your password.
No link to click, no urgency, and it tells you to log in through the bank's own app or site. That's how legitimate notices work.
Text message
VERIFY
Your verification code is 472913. Don't share this code with anyone.
A code you just requested is normal and safe to use yourself — but never read or forward it to anyone who calls or messages asking for it.
2

Pop-ups & Phone Calls

What shows up on your screen or comes through on a call.

4 examples
Pop-up window
⚠ Security Alert
Your computer is infected with 5 viruses! Call Microsoft Support now at 1-800-555-0190 to remove them immediately.
Real virus warnings never tell you to call a phone number. This is a fake pop-up. Don't call — close the window or restart your browser.
Phone call
Unknown caller
“This is the IRS. You owe back taxes. Pay immediately with gift cards or a warrant will be issued for your arrest today.”
Government agencies never demand gift cards and never threaten arrest by phone. Hang up — it's a scam.
Phone call
Corner Pharmacy
“Hello, this is a reminder that your prescription is ready for pickup anytime this week. No need to call back. Thank you!”
It only reminds you to pick something up and asks for nothing — no payment, no personal information. That's a normal reminder.
Phone call
Your bank's fraud line
“We noticed an unusual charge. We'll never ask for your PIN or password — please call the number on the back of your card to confirm.”
It tells you to call the number on your own card and says it will never ask for your PIN. That's how a real bank behaves.
