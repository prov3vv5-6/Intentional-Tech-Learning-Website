Getting Into the Call

Joining the coaching session when it's time.

3 questions
It's time for your coaching session on Google Meet. How do you join?
The invite has a link — tap it at the start time and it opens the call. If you can't find it, ask your coach to text or email it again.
A box pops up asking to use your microphone and camera. What should you do?
This is normal and safe for a video call — without it, no one can see or hear you. You can always mute or stop your video once you're in.
The screen says “Ask to join” and then “Waiting for the host.” Is something wrong?
This is completely normal. The coach simply admits you into the call. Sit tight for a few seconds — there's nothing to fix.

Once You're In

The handful of buttons you'll actually use during a call.

4 questions
The coach says “I can't hear you.” The microphone button is red with a line through it. What does that mean, and what do you do?
A red microphone with a line through it means you're muted. One tap turns it back on so the coach can hear you.
Someone comes to the door and you need a moment where you're not on camera. What's the easiest thing to do?
Turning the camera off pauses your video without leaving the call. Tap it again when you're ready to be seen. You can mute too, if you like.
The coach asks, “Can you show me what you're seeing on your screen?” What do you do?
Sharing your screen lets the coach see exactly what's on your device, so they can guide you step by step. Tap “Present now,” choose your screen, and tap it again to stop when you're done.
The session is over and you're ready to go. How do you leave the call properly?
The red phone button hangs up cleanly and closes the call. Walking away leaves you connected, so always tap it to finish.
